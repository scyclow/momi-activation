WHAT’S ON

Marty Supreme (with Ronald Bronstein in person)

January 23, 2026

Set in post–WWII New York, this breathless ride from Josh Safdie follows cocky Jewish ping-pong whiz kid Marty Mauser (Chalamet) as he charts a wild, Saul Bellow-like odyssey from the Lower East Side to Tokyo, scheming and scamming his way from one unimaginable predicament to the next, and pissing off just about everyone he meets.

Videoheaven (with director Alex Ross Perry and editor Clyde Folley in person)

January 24, 2026

In this giddily overstuffed cine-essay more than a decade in the making, filmmaker Alex Ross Perry (Listen Up Phillip) has created a maniacally thorough, hugely entertaining look at the legacy of the video store in culture and as it’s been represented on film and television.

One Battle After Another in 70mm

January 24–25, 2026

Sixteen years after going into witness protection, former revolutionary activist Bob Ferguson (Leonardo DiCaprio) is thrust back into reluctant action when his teenage daughter Willa (Chase Infiniti) becomes the target of a relentless military colonel (Sean Penn) with a very personal grudge. See the year's most acclaimed film in special 70mm screenings!

Tale of Tales: The Films of Yuri Norstein

January 23 & 31, 2026

The legendary Russian animator Yuri Norstein, a master of cut-out animation, is often cited as a major influence by Hayao Miyazaki and many other great contemporary filmmakers. Thanks to new restorations, this program features beloved Norstein shorts, plus early works, in a program spanning 1968–1979.

Peter Hujar’s Day (with director Ira Sachs in person)

February 7, 2026

A delicate and moving portrayal of a sliver of time in the life of groundbreaking gay photographer Peter Hujar, based on rediscovered transcripts from an unused 1974 interview by nonfiction writer Linda Rosenkrantz.
